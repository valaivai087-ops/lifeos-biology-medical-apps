🧬 基本概念 | Basic Concepts ▼
📖 解剖學
解剖學（Anatomy）是研究動物的物理結構。
🔊 🔊 Anatomy
Anatomy is the study of an animal's physical structure.
📖 生理學
生理學（Physiology）是研究物理結構如何在生物體中發揮功能。
🔊 🔊 Physiology
Physiology is the study of how physical structures function in an organism.
🔑 核心原理
比較性研究表明，形態和功能密切相關；不同的組織有不同的結構以適應其功能。
🔊 🔊 Core Principle
Comparative studies reveal that form and function are closely correlated; different tissues have different structures suited to their functions.
🧵 四種主要動物組織
Four Main Types of Animal Tissues
🛡️ 上皮組織 | Epithelial Tissue ▼
功能
覆蓋身體外部，並排列在身體內的器官和空腔內。
🔊 🔊 Function
Covers the outside of the body and lines organs and cavities within the body.
🔷 細胞形狀
上皮細胞的形狀可分為三種：
🔊 🔊 Cell Shape
The shape of epithelial cells can be classified into three types:
  • 立方型 | Cuboidal - 正方形排列 | Square arrangement
  • 柱狀 | Columnar - 高柱形細胞 | Tall column-shaped cells
  • 鱗狀 | Squamous - 扁平細胞 | Flattened cells
🏗️ 細胞排列方式
上皮細胞的排列方式可分為三種：
🔊 🔊 Cell Arrangement
The arrangement of epithelial cells can be classified into three types:
  • 單層 | Simple - 單層細胞組成 | Single cell layer
  • 多層 | Stratified - 多層細胞組成 | Multiple cell layers
  • 假多層 | Pseudostratified - 看起來像多層但實際只有一層 | Appears multilayered but actually single layer
⚙️ 結構特性
上皮細胞緊密相接，具有極性（分別面向體內和體外環境），並缺乏血管但有神經分佈。
🔊 🔊 Structural Features
Epithelial cells are tightly joined, have polarity facing internal and external environments, and lack blood vessels but have nerve distribution.
🔗 結締組織 | Connective Tissue ▼
功能
包含稀疏分散在結締組織基質中的細胞，主要用於綁定和支持。
🔊 🔊 Function
Contains sparsely packed cells scattered throughout a matrix, mainly used for binding and support.
🧵 三種纖維類型
結締組織中的蛋白質纖維有三種：
🔊 🔊 Three Types of Fibers
There are three types of protein fibers in connective tissue:
  • 膠原纖維 | Collagenous Fibers - 含膠原蛋白，提供強度，未分支 | Contain collagen, provide strength, unbranched
  • 網狀纖維 | Reticular Fibers - 細而高度分支，形成網絡 | Thin and highly branched, form networks
  • 彈性纖維 | Elastic Fibers - 含彈性蛋白，具有彈性 | Contain elastin, provide elasticity
🏥 主要類型
  • 疏鬆結締組織 | Loose Connective Tissue - 綁定並支持其他組織
  • 纖維結締組織 | Fibrous Connective Tissue - 肌腱和韌帶中發現
  • 脂肪組織 | Adipose Tissue - 儲存能量並提供絕緣
  • 軟骨 | Cartilage - 強韌且有彈性的支持材料
  • 骨 | Bone - 最堅硬的結締組織，形成骨骼
  • 血液 | Blood - 細胞嵌入液體基質中
🔊 🔊 Main Types
💪 肌肉組織 | Muscle Tissue ▼
功能
由肌肉纖維組成，響應神經信號而收縮，在運動中發揮作用。
🔊 🔊 Function
Consists of muscle fibers that contract in response to nerve signals and function in movement.
🏋️ 三種肌肉類型
  • 骨骼肌 | Skeletal Muscle
    自主控制 | Voluntary control | 長條紋纖維 | Striated fibers | 附著在骨骼上 | Attached to bones
  • 平滑肌 | Smooth Muscle
    非自主控制 | Involuntary control | 無紋理 | Non-striated | 位於血管和內臟 | Found in blood vessels and organs
  • 心肌 | Cardiac Muscle
    非自主控制 | Involuntary control | 有紋理 | Striated | 形成心臟 | Forms the heart
🔊 🔊 Three Types
肌動蛋白和肌球蛋白
骨骼肌細胞包含專門的蛋白質細絲。肌動蛋白（薄細絲）和肌球蛋白（厚細絲）相互作用以響應磷酸化而移動。

🔊 🔊 Actin and Myosin:
Skeletal muscle cells contain specialized protein filaments. Actin thin filaments and myosin thick filaments interact to move in response to phosphorylation.
🧠 神經組織 | Nervous Tissue ▼
功能
感知刺激並在全身傳導信號。
🔊 🔊 Function
Senses stimuli and transmits signals throughout the animal body.
🔌 主要組成
神經組織由神經元和神經支持細胞組成：
🔊 🔊 Main Components
Nervous tissue consists of neurons and supporting cells:
  • 神經元 | Neurons - 傳輸神經脈衝的神經細胞
    • 樹突 | Dendrites - 接收信號 | Receive signals
    • 胞體 | Cell body - 包含核 | Contains nucleus
    • 軸突 | Axon - 發送信號 | Transmit signals
  • 神經支持細胞 | Support Cells - 支持和絕緣神經元
突觸傳遞
神經末梢和另一細胞膜之間的突觸間隙約50nm。當神經衝動到達軸突末端時，神經傳遞物質被釋放到突觸間隙中。

🔊 🔊 Synaptic Transmission:
The synaptic gap between an axon terminal and another cell membrane is about fifty nanometers. When a nerve impulse reaches the axon terminal, neurotransmitters are released into the synaptic gap.
⚖️ 第二章：體內平衡與調節
Chapter 2: Homeostasis and Regulation
⚙️ 體內平衡 | Homeostasis ▼
定義
恆定是維持動物細胞、組織和器官的相對恆定的化學和物理條件或"穩定狀態"的維持。
🔊 🔊 Definition
Homeostasis is the maintenance of relatively constant chemical and physical conditions or steady state in an animal's cells, tissues, and organs.
🔄 調節機制
  • 正反饋 | Positive Feedback - 放大信號或反應。例如：分娩期間子宮收縮增加 | Amplifies signal or response. Example: Increased uterine contractions during labor
  • 負反饋 | Negative Feedback - 主要恆定機制，使變量接近設定值。例如：體溫調節 | Primary homeostatic mechanism, keeps variable near set point. Example: Temperature regulation
🔊 🔊 Regulatory Mechanisms
🫀 器官系統對恆定的貢獻
  • 消化系統 | Digestive System - 食物處理（攝取、消化、吸收、排除）
  • 呼吸系統 | Respiratory System - 氧氣/二氧化碳交換
  • 肝臟 | Liver - 血糖/胰島素調節血糖水平
  • 腎臟 | Kidneys - 滲透壓、pH調節
🔊 🔊 Organ System Contributions
🌡️ 體溫調節 | Thermoregulation ▼
🦎 動物分類
內溫動物（恆溫動物）保持恆定的體溫；外溫動物（變溫動物）經歷身體溫度隨環境變化。
🔊 🔊 Animal Classification
Endotherms keep their body temperature constant; ectotherms experience changes in body temperature with their environment.
🛡️ 五種適應方式
幫助動物體溫調節的五種適應方式：
🔊 🔊 Five Adaptations
There are five main adaptations that help animals thermoregulate:
  • 隔離（絕緣）| Insulation - 哺乳動物和鳥類通過皮膚、毛髮和羽毛進行隔離
  • 蒸發冷卻 | Evaporative Heat Loss - 許多動物通過皮膚蒸發失去熱量
  • 循環適應 | Circulatory Adaptations - 動物具有逆流熱交換器
  • 行為反應 | Behavioral Responses - 内溫和外溫動物都使用行為反應
  • 代謝熱生成 | Metabolic Heat Production - 肌肉活動或非顫抖產熱
📐 第三章：動物大小、形態與進化
Chapter 3: Animal Size, Form and Evolution
📏 表面積與體積的關係 | Surface Area-to-Volume Ratio ▼
核心原理
隨著細胞變大，其體積增加速度遠快於其表面積。因此，當細胞變大時，跨越細胞膜發生的運輸過程必須支持不成比例地增加更多的細胞體積。
🔊 🔊 Core Principle
As a cell gets larger, its volume increases much faster than its surface area. Transport processes across the cell membrane must support disproportionately more cell volume as cell size increases.
例子 | Example：
鰓具有扁平的薄膜狀結構（鰓片），器官具有極高的表面積與體積比。擴散可以快速進行，足以跟上發育中魚類體積的增長。

🔊 🔊
Gills have flattened sheet-like structures that provide extremely high surface area to volume ratios. Diffusion can occur rapidly enough to keep up with volume growth in developing fish.

英文 | English：
You measure body temperatures of desert animals at 7 AM and again at 2 PM. Which animal is most likely endothermic?

中文 | Chinese：
你在上午7點和下午2點測量沙漠動物的體溫。哪種動物最可能是內溫動物？
Animal | 動物 7 AM | 上午7點 2 PM | 下午2點
Snake | 蛇 77° F | 25° C 87° F | 31° C
Mouse | 小鼠 100° F | 37.8° C 99° F | 37.2° C
Lizard | 蜥蜴 80° F | 26.7° C 105° F | 40.6° C
Beetle | 甲蟲 72° F | 22.2° C 102° F | 38.9° C
A) Snake | 蛇
B) Mouse | 小鼠
C) Lizard | 蜥蜴
D) Beetle | 甲蟲
✓ 正確答案 | Correct Answer: B) Mouse
小鼠的體溫在上午和下午之間保持相對恆定（100°F 到 99°F），這表明它是內溫動物（產生自己的熱量）。相比之下，蛇、蜥蜴和甲蟲的溫度隨著環境溫度的變化而變化，表明它們是外溫動物。
🔊 🔊 The mouse maintains relatively constant body temperature between morning and afternoon, which indicates it is an endotherm that produces its own heat. In contrast, the snake, lizard, and beetle show temperature changes following environmental temperature changes, indicating they are ectotherms.

A) Endothermic homeotherm | 內溫恆溫動物
B) Endothermic poikilotherm | 內溫變溫動物

🔊 🔊 Sea stars are ectothermic animals that do not produce their own heat, but because they live in the deep sea where temperature is stable year-round at 4 degrees Celsius, they are effectively homeothermic. They maintain constant body temperature without active regulation because their environment is already stable.
